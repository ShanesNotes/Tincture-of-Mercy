# Memoir vs Mythic Transmutation Boundaries

Status: Phase 7 — per-scene boundary ledger  
Owner lane: narrative ethics + story production  
Companion: `STORYBOARD_BIBLE.md` §3, `CONSOLIDATED_LORE_SURFACE.md` § Lived-source transmutation  
Created: 2026-06-11

## Default mode

**Mythic transmutation** — lived autobiographical material (COVID bedside, Corewell/GR healthcare, loss, addiction/recovery) becomes Michigan post-Turn pilgrimage imagery. Not literal memoir unless a row below says **literal opt-in**.

## Boundary vocabulary

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **mythic** | Fully transmuted; no real institution/person identifiable |
| **mythic-leaning** | Texture from lived experience; no proper names or identifiable rooms |
| **hybrid** | Specific professional texture allowed (e.g. chart language) without real patient stories |
| **literal opt-in** | Requires explicit author decision before production |
| **forbidden** | Do not mine without permission/transform |

## Global guardrails (all scenes)

- Do not cast real patients, colleagues, or family as characters without permission and transformation.
- Do not frame addiction as villainy; Ember is effective relief with cost.
- Do not frame healthcare workers as villains; Wittehaven is partly right.
- Do not reduce grief to content; every scene must justify personhood or mercy.
- Bethany payoff = recognition/presence, not recipe victory.

## Production checklist (per new scene card)

1. Assign **mode** from table above before writing image/VO prompts.
2. If **hybrid**, strip proper nouns of real institutions and people.
3. If scene needs **literal opt-in**, stop and mark card `BLOCKED: literal review required`.
4. Cross-check `STORYBOARD_BIBLE.md` §4 canon guardrails.
5. Log new scenes in this table when adding cards.
