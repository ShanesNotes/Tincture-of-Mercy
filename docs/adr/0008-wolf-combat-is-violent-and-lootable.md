# Wolf combat is violent and lootable

The wolf encounter is real combat. Kalev can use violence to protect the child and himself, and wolves may leave recoverable loot/materials. Normal RPG risk/reward applies: danger, combat cost, resource use, drops, materials, and progression hooks are mechanically meaningful. The encounter objective remains protection and escape: the boy reaching safety resolves the encounter, while loot is part of the material economy rather than the definition of victory.

Considered Options:
- Remove loot from the wolf encounter: rejected because it overcorrects into a moralized rule the active direction does not need.
- Make wolf loot the encounter objective: rejected because it would displace the protection/escape premise.
- Allow lethal, lootable combat while keeping boy safety as the objective: chosen because it preserves first-class combat without losing the scene's mercy frame.
- Treat combat as only symbolic risk: rejected because the WoW-shaped substrate exists to support normal RPG mechanics and risk/reward.
