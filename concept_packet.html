§ 2 · Foundation

The Ten Pillars · v0.9

Non-negotiable. If a proposed feature contradicts a pillar, it is not built. The pillars are the ceiling, not the floor.

Pillar 01

One event truth

Care, combat, craft, witness, economy, notebook, and aftermath are projections over the same SimEvent stream. Presenters can hide or rename fields; they do not own outcome state.

Pillar 02

One resolver family

OutcomeResolver and combat tables share one path. Combat is data, not a second engine. Care and combat fixtures use the same resolver assertion.

Pillar 03

Substrate before content

M1 core mechanics pass headless tests before M3 opening acts begin. Epic C carries blocked_by: [B0] until B1–B6 land.

Pillar 04

Combat is first-class

Wolves are real. They can harm Kalev or Iiro. Threat, damage, cooldowns, loot/material consequence, and progression hooks all carry acceptance criteria — not symbolic flavor.

Pillar 05

Care remains embodied

Water, bread, tincture, presence, and witness are actions under time, resource, and attention pressure. Bedside vocabulary stays tactile and person-specific.

Pillar 06

Death has gravity

Act 4 Anna witness/death is a fixed-outcome teaching encounter. The player acts meaningfully; the substrate learns; death cannot be converted into control.

Pillar 07

Names over scoreboards

The notebook records persons, page, line, and consequence. XP-style accounting can exist as internal/debug projection; player-facing surfaces stay register-appropriate.

Pillar 08

Recognition, not recipe victory

Iiro remembers Kalev's presence. Corrected recipe is Apothecary learning that may improve later practice but does not cause the Bethany payoff.

Pillar 09

Tuning levers, not moral bans

Scarcity, diminishing returns, danger, route risk, and pacing shape farming. No active rule erases loot/material reward from violent combat as doctrine.

Pillar 10

Godot 4.6 · C# · Forward+

Engine constraints stay. Implementation remains small, readable, data-driven, replayable, headlessly testable.

§ 6 · The Foundation

Substrate Primitives

The seventeen contracts that ship and pass headless tests before opening act content. Build order is strict: clock → events → resolver → state → encounter math → timers → data → loot → death → progression → presenters. Each primitive is event-derived and replayable.

1 · SimClock

Fixed-tick scheduler. Pause, resume, time scale, seed. Deterministic replay foundation.

tick · time_scale · seed · phase

2 · SimEvent stream

Append-only ring buffer. The authoritative record. Every system reads here; presenters do not own outcome state.

id · tick · actor · target · verb · domain · cost · result · tags

3 · OutcomeResolver

One resolver family for care, craft, combat, witness, path/receptivity. CombatTable.Resolve() is data, not a parallel engine. Scripted fixtures and seeded rolls supported.

roll_kind · table_id · modifiers · outcome

4 · AuraSystem

Add / refresh / stack / expire. Used by tinctures, wolf wounds, Witness aftermath.

aura_id · target · duration_ticks · modifier_ids

5 · ActorState / StatBlock / DerivedStats

Actor model with derived stats from base + equipment + auras + burdens. Health, Spirit, Steady, Burden, Pressure, Numbness all flow through here.

stat_key · old_value · new_value · reason

6 · DisparityService

Encounter and care mismatch math. Contributes risk modifiers; does not own outcomes.

disparity_kind · source · target · modifier

7 · AttentionThreatTable

Care & combat threat. Wolf target choice between Kalev and Iiro: proximity, attacks, wounds, noise, care focus.

threat_actor · delta · reason · current_target

8 · AggroCallFleeRadius

Encounter AI radii. Aggro enter/exit, pack call escalation, flee route, leash return.

radius_id · trigger · target · route_id

9 · Timer suite

GCD, work / cast / channel, swing, rite pulse. Start, tick, complete, interrupted — all observable events.

timer_kind · duration_ticks · interrupted_by

10 · Resource profiles

Health / Spirit / Steady / Burden / Pressure / Numbness costs and consequences, all event-derived.

resource_key · delta · cause

11 · FSR / Vigil cooldown

Kalev self-administration. Tactical benefit (Pressure relief, Steady) vs. cost (Numbness, Burden, cooldown debt).

cooldown_id · started_tick · ready_tick · benefit · cost

12 · AbilityDef / VerbDef

Verb data rows: tags, costs, timer, resolver table, presenter key. All acts use the same shape.

verb_id · cost_keys · timer_id · table_id · presenter_key

13 · ItemDef / quality / loot hooks

Item defs, loot tables, quality budget, source-actor binding. Bread, tincture ingredients, wolf material — same accounting.

item_id · quality · rarity · loot_table · source_actor

14 · Leash / route / respawn

Encounter route graph, leash radius, reset rules, death/down state. Wolves can flee/leash without dying.

route_id · node · leash_state · reset_reason

15 · Death / friction / moral death

Anna fixed-outcome death (gravity encounter). Kalev down/death per tuning. Wolf death enables loot eligibility. Recovery friction is a knob, not a moral.

death_kind · cause_event · recoverable · witness_hook

16 · Progression state

Witness, Recollection, Vocation/path points. XP-style projection where scoped, internal/debug-first. All event-derived.

progression_key · delta · source_event · path

17 · Notebook + domain presenters

Read-only views. Notebook, bedside, combat, economy, debug. Presenters can hide or rename fields; they project events, never own them.

presenter_id · source_event_ids · surface · copy_key

Illustrative shape

{

    IReadOnlyList<string> Tags

§ 7 · The Three Attentions

Latent Paths & Receptivity

Three growth disciplines, distinct from voice registers. Paths shape what Kalev notices; registers shape what surfaces and speakers say. Hesychasm is the active path name; Pastoral is a historical/source alias only.

Apothecary

Bodies, ingredients, dosing, pressure, practical sequence.

Verbs: observe symptoms · wash · warm · prepare/administer tincture · bind · ration · break/offer bread.

Costs: technical tunnel vision, missed presence cue, over-trusting treatment.

events: craft.* · care.* · resolver metadata

Hesychasm

Watchfulness, prayerful presence, breath, fear, bearing witness.

Verbs: sit near · keep watch · speak name · pray · wait for breath · hold hand · witness.

Costs: time, Spirit, vulnerability during danger, carried Burden.

events: presence.* · witness.* · recognition seed

Iconographic

Image, pattern, symbol, composition, sacred disclosure through matter.

Verbs: notice image · arrange object · trace sign · mark page · read threshold · recognize.

Costs: misreading symbol, distraction from bodily need, over-interpretation.

events: iconographic.* · notebook.mark_image · recollection hook

Receptivity contract

ReceptivityProfile describes what a person can receive at a given moment. register_match_modifier is the resolver-facing score derived from action register, target receptivity, path state, and context. The same atoms drive bedside fit, recognition seeds, and Bethany payoff.

§ 9 · Three Worlds

The Three Regimes

The same UI atoms render under three grammars — the visual law from v0.8.1 stays active under v0.9. The geometry does not move; the grammar inverts. The opening five-act slice lives in the Ironwood register; Wittehaven and Paradise are scoped to later phases.

Ironwood  ·  Folk Mercy

Rough, damp, dignified; every surface worn but readable.

Hand-script labels. Notebook hand. Tactile symptom words: thin, cold, quiet, fading. Names in IM Fell English. The Tincture Wheel is hand-drawn and humble. Earth tones; one ember-red glint when the vial is out.

Wittehaven · Sanctioned Mercy

The relief of clean grids and clear answers — the seduction that becomes control.

ALL-CAPS case IDs in IBM Plex Mono. Patients become stages, dosing, radiation levels. The Wheel becomes clean, exact, protocol-scored. Zero corner radius. Sharp shadows. Foreshadow only in P0.

Paradise · Sacred Mercy

Recognition, not reward. Names already known. The practice that survives.

Cinzel sacred display. Gold halo on the cedar dog. Names on the wall. Kalev's name written by another hand. P2 reserved — IC XC NIKA, halos, narthex are not in the prototype.

Gold appears only when something eternal presses through. Red appears only when fire is live, dangerous, or accusatory. The grammar inverts; the geometry does not.

§ 13 · The Vertical Slice

The Five-Act Opening

The opening proves the substrate in play. Each act names the primitives it teaches, the events it must record, and the debug-acceptance that proves it. Implementation is blocked_by: [B0] until M1 substrate core lands.

Act 1 · Water

Embodied care as action under time and state pressure.

  • Verbs: observe · fetch water · wash face/hands · cool cloth · warm hands / stoke fire · sit near · write name seed.
  • Taught primitives: SimClock · SimEvent · ActorState · resource profiles (Pressure / Burden / Steady) · work timer · notebook presenter.
  • Authored events: act1.enter_cabin · care.observe.mother · care.water.use · care.warmth.adjust · notebook.name_seed.
  • Outcomes: ordered care eases Anna comfort and Iiro trust seed; Kalev Steady stabilizes. No hard fail by itself — the act teaches consequence, not a fail screen.
  • Debug acceptance: replay fixture shows observe → water-use in tick order; comfort/trust projections derive from events; no scene-local variable owns the final state.

Act 2 · Bread

Ordinary mercy under constraint — food, timing, receptivity, consequence. Not a recipe puzzle. Bread, not porridge.

  • Verbs: check food · break bread · offer (to Iiro or Anna) · moisten · wait for breath · save for later.
  • Taught primitives: ItemDef · work/channel timer · ReceptivityProfile · register_match_modifier · economy event · notebook presenter for ordinary care.
  • Authored events: item.bread.inspect · care.bread.break · care.bread.offer · state.receptivity.shift · notebook.ordinary_mercy.
  • Outcomes: fitting offer improves receptivity, eases pressure, strengthens child trust seed (a Bethany recognition hook). Poor timing wastes scarce food.
  • Debug acceptance: bread inventory only changes through item.bread.* / care.bread.*; receptivity shifts cite their source event; act data and UI use bread.

Act 3 · Tincture

The shared resolver becomes visible. Medicine is real and costly without becoming the final meaning of care.

  • Verbs: inspect · grind / steep / measure · administer · hold back · self-administer (FSR / Vigil) · record preparation.
  • Taught primitives: OutcomeResolver (scripted fixture + seeded roll) · AbilityDef / VerbDef · AuraSystem · FSR/Vigil cooldown · DisparityService · resource profiles (Spirit / Steady / Numbness / Pressure).
  • Authored events: craft.tincture.prepare · resolver.tincture.roll · care.tincture.administer · aura.tincture.apply · fsr_vigil.self_use · notebook.tincture_record.
  • Outcomes: clean prep / admin = clearer state and good debug trace. Rough = pressure / burden cost. Self-use = tactical benefit + later cost (Numbness rises, Recollection consequence).
  • Debug acceptance: resolver event includes table id, modifiers, roll/fixture metadata, outcome. Care + later combat fixtures share the same resolver family. Self-use emits both benefit and consequence.

Act 4 · Anna death / Witness gravity encounter

Inverse first-boss shape. Player acts meaningfully, learns mechanics, and cannot convert care into control over death. The outcome is fixed; the mechanics are real.

  • Verbs: observe breath · administer (if remains) · sit near / hold hand · speak name (Hesychasm) · pray / keep watch · draw sign / notice image (Iconographic) · witness · write name.
  • Taught primitives: death / friction / moral death · Witness & Recollection progression · path / receptivity hooks · resolver fixture with fixed outcome · notebook presenter.
  • Authored events: care.last_actions · resolver.death_fixture · death.mother · witness.kalev · notebook.write_name · progression.recollection_seed.
  • Outcomes: Anna dies in the authored event. Player actions affect comfort tags, Iiro fear/trust and recognition seed, Kalev Burden & Witness, notebook prose, Numbness drift.
  • Debug acceptance: death event carries fixed-outcome metadata; player actions affect ≥3 projections (comfort, Kalev resources, Iiro seed, notebook/progression); the act is not a non-interactive cutscene.

First-class combat under the shared substrate. Real danger, threat, violence, cost, possible loot. Encounter objective: Iiro reaches safety. Replay runs this beat before Act 4 witness.

§ 14 · The Reward Surface

RPG Economy & Progression

Combat is real, so reward is real. The economy principle: encounter objective and material consequence are separate. Iiro reaching safety resolves the wolf encounter; loot follows from the event stream, not from doctrine. Tuning is allowed; moralized bans are not.

Reward channels

channel 01

Loot / material

loot.* + combat.wolf.death_or_flee + gather events. Wolf hide, sinew, tooth/claw, meat, blood-risk per tuning.

channel 02

Equipment

Item/equip events with quality tags. Later build expression and survival preparation: protective wrap, knife, tool hooks if scoped.

channel 03

Witness

Death / protection / presence events. Carried consequence and theological/narrative memory. Can be reward and burden.

channel 04

Recollection

Notebook / witness / path events. Bethany recognition seeds. Names, page, line, prior protection.

channel 05

Vocation / path points

Apothecary, Hesychasm, Iconographic growth from path-tagged events. Unlocks readings, lowers costs, changes receptivity fit.

channel 06

XP-style projection

Internal / debug-facing where scoped. Player-facing copy uses register-fitting words: notebook, recollection, vocation, practice, wound, named memory.

Item quality & rarity

Internal architecture term → tactile player-facing mapping:

poor
torn · fouled · cracked. Useful for gritty salvage.
rough
rough · usable · smoke-stained. Default for opening wolf material.
sound
sound · clean · kept. Strong ordinary quality.
fine
well-cut · steady · close-grained. Earned, not flashy.
rare
uncommon · hard-won · marked. Use carefully on world surfaces.
legendary
named relic · remembered thing · singular piece. Architecture concept; presentation must fit the game.

Tuning levers, not moral bans

  • Avoid shallow farming dominance: diminishing returns · scarcity · danger · time pressure · patrol changes. "This route's wolves stop producing useful hide after the body is spoiled or the pack scatters."
  • Keep protection objective central: objective reward separate from loot. "Boy safety resolves the encounter; loot is a material consequence."
  • Prevent infinite safety: wounds · cooldowns · pressure · route risk · leash/call behavior. "Repeated fighting raises danger and cost."
  • Keep care meaningful: attention/resource opportunity costs. "Time spent looting may affect the next bedside beat."
  • Preserve notebook tone: presenter mapping. "Debug may show XP; notebook records names and burdens."
Looting takes time and attention. Materials enable later care or survival. Combat wounds create care needs. Bread, water, tincture, and wolf materials all use the same inventory and event accounting. The economy intensifies care; it does not replace it.

§ 16 · The Strict

Active Locks · v0.9

Substrate

  • One event truth: SimEvent is the authoritative record for every consequential action.
  • One resolver family: OutcomeResolver + CombatTable.Resolve(). No separate care/combat resolver.
  • Substrate before content: M1 core mechanics tested before M3 opening acts. Epic C carries blocked_by: [B0].
  • Domain presenters read events; they do not own outcome state.

Combat

  • First-class. Wolves can harm Kalev or Iiro. Threat, damage, cooldowns, loot/material consequence, progression hooks all carry acceptance.
  • Encounter objective: Iiro safety. Loot is grounded consequence, not victory condition.
  • Scoped vocabulary in active docs and debug: combat · damage · threat · aggro · attack table · GCD · swing/cast/channel timer · loot · loot table · material · rarity · quality · equipment · XP-style accounting · boss/gravity encounter · death/friction.

Care

  • Bedside vocabulary stays tactile and person-specific: warm · dry · steady · carried · fading · write name.
  • Notebook is append-only, persistent, person-specific. Not a card collection. Not a quest log.
  • Bethany payoff = recognition / presence. Corrected recipe is subordinate Apothecary learning, never the salvific cause.

Resources

  • Six event-derived: Health · Spirit · Steady · Burden · Pressure · Numbness.
  • Player-facing surfaces map raw values to register-fitting language; debug surfaces show numbers directly.
  • FSR/Vigil self-use trades Pressure for Numbness with cooldown and aftermath cost.

Paths

  • Three latent paths: Apothecary · Hesychasm · Iconographic.
  • Hesychasm is the active name. Pastoral is a historical/source alias only.
  • Use paths, not skills.
  • Voice registers (folk · sanctioned · sacred) ≠ latent paths. Registers govern copy; paths govern attention/practice.
  • Receptivity is data: ReceptivityProfile + register_match_modifier feed the resolver.
