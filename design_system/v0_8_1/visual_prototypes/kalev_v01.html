§ 01Direction

Kalev is the only person the player will move for the entire prototype slice. Everything we ask of his image must read in pure black silhouette at native scale, in two seconds, in candlelight. He is not a hero. He is a man with a leather notebook, a small carved cedar dog, and one glass vial of Ember he should not drink. The body has to carry that load before any frame of animation does.

The visual register is Michigan icon-manuscript pixel art — woodcut contour, three garment colors, a long cedar coat that reads as a vertical column from far away, a damp wool scarf that breaks the column off-center so he doesn't read symmetric or noble. He is asymmetric. The hem is rough. The boots are heavy. The shoulders are 1 pixel low on his left: that asymmetry is the Grey Stone, made into geometry.

What makes him memorable is not ornament. It is what he carries: a square corner at the right hip — the leather-bound notebook with a single brass clasp, the only metal on his body — and at the left chest, the slight bulge where the cedar dog rests inside the coat. The Ember vial is not visible at idle; it lives at the inside-belt and only appears in his hand during the Administer animation. He looks like a working nurse who has begun to read the world as a manuscript and is not yet sure that's wise.

§ 07What Kalev is — and is not

Do

  • A working body. Knees bent at idle. Shoulders not square. Boots planted.
  • Silhouette before colour. Cedar column + scarf break + hip corner. In that order.
  • Three garment colours. Cedar, lake-slate, vellum-bone. Plus skin, hair, ink.
  • One brass pixel. The clasp. That is all the metal in this entire prototype slice.
  • Asymmetry as language. Low left shoulder. Off-centre scarf. Uneven dog ear inside the coat.
  • Hidden Ember. The vial only appears during use. You should not be able to point to where it lives.
  • Quiet eyes. Two single ink dots. Direction comes from posture and head turn, not pupils.

Do not

  • No cape. No flowing fabric. Wool, not silk. The only break in the column is the scarf.
  • No glow on Kalev. He does not radiate. Only the Ember vial glows, and only in his hand.
  • No weapon. No knife on the belt, no walking stick, no rifle. He is a healer.
  • No badges, no insignia. No nurse symbol, no plus, no rank. Wittehaven would put one on him; we won't.
  • No emoji-face. No smile pixels. No frown. The mouth is implied by beard, not drawn.
  • No four-color garment. If you find yourself wanting an accent, kill it. The brass clasp is the budget.
  • No symmetry. If both shoulders are level, the design has failed.
