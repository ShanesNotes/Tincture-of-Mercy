# Sprite-sheet prompt — Wolf · 96×64

Status: active authoring prompt · new in v0.9
Owner lane: art direction + production
Sheet target: `art/characters/wolves/wolf_full_sheet_96x64.png`
Active in: Act 5 (wolves / boy flight / combat). First combat actor in the project.
Canvas decision rationale: 96×64 is wider-than-tall because wolves are quadrupeds longer than they are tall. The lunge/call/flee poses extend across the full 96px length. This canvas size is new in v0.9 and should be added to `design_system/v0_8_1/godot_asset_manifest_v0_8_1.md` §6.2 as a v0.9 amendment when manifest gets its v0.9 update pass.

## Prompt

```text
You are producing the runtime sprite sheet for a Wolf, the Act 5 hostile encounter actor in *Tincture of Mercy: The Garments of Skin* — a combat-capable mercy RPG vertical slice in Godot 4.6 / C# / Forward+. Authoring rules below are non-negotiable.

CHARACTER
- A real wolf — predator, frightened of humans only when out-mass-ed, lethal to a child. Pack animal. Kalev's first combat encounter exists to prove combat is real, costly, and grounded.
- The sprite has to support: idle, four-direction walk, pack-call howl, lunge attack, bite, hit-react, flee, and death. The encounter objective is the boy reaching safety; the wolf can be killed, fled, or leashed and still resolve the encounter.
- Silhouette laws (must read at 2× zoom):
  · long-bodied quadruped, withers ~40 px tall in idle pose, body length ~60 px nose-to-tail-base
  · low head carriage in idle; head and neck level with or below the back line
  · long bushy tail hanging low in idle; rises in alert/call frames; goes between legs in flee frames
  · ears alert and forward in idle/walk; pinned back in lunge/bite; flat in hit-react
  · visible spine/shoulder definition (not chunky stylization)
  · pads of feet visible on the ground — feet are 2-pixel clusters at the canvas baseline
  · in `lunge` and `bite` frames: full body extends to occupy the 96px width — front paws lifted, rear paws pushed off
  · in `pack_call` frame: head lifted toward upper edge of canvas, neck vertical, posture compressed
- Avoid: anthropomorphism, "cute" eyes, oversized teeth/snarl-monster, glowing eyes, magical aura, dire-wolf fantasy scale, cartoon villain features, slime/blood splatter on the wolf body itself (combat consequence shows through Kalev's animations and notebook prose, not as gore on the wolf sprite).

CANVAS
- 96×64 pixels per frame
- 5 columns × 8 rows = 480 px wide × 512 px tall, transparent PNG, RGBA
- No background, no grid lines, no labels, no guide rulers
- Foot baseline y = 60 in idle/walk/call/hit-react/flee/death rows (paws on the ground)
- Lunge/bite rows: rear paws may push off, so anchor varies — the leaping center-of-mass should still imply a return to baseline y = 60

ANIMATION ROWS
  Row 0: idle,            2 frames + 3 empty cells — slow breath + ear flick variant
  Row 1: walk_down,       4 frames + 1 empty — quadruped walk gait, two-beat pattern
  Row 2: walk_left,       4 frames + 1 empty
  Row 3: walk_up,         4 frames + 1 empty
  Row 4: walk_right,      4 frames + 1 empty
  Row 5: pack_call,       3 frames + 2 empty — head down → head up → held vertical (the howl)
  Row 6: lunge,           4 frames + bite 1 (= 5 frames)
    · lunge frames 1–4: crouch → push-off → mid-air extension → landing front-paws-first
    · bite frame 5: head-and-shoulders thrust at target, ears pinned
  Row 7: hit_react 2 + flee_loop 2 + death 1 (= 5 frames in one row)
    · hit_react frames 1–2: body flinches; ears flat; tail drops
    · flee_loop frames 3–4: tail tucked between legs, low gallop posture; loops with walk frames inverted-direction at runtime
    · death frame 5: collapsed on side, legs folded under; this is the canonical "body recoverable" pose for `loot.wolf.material` events

PIXEL-ART RIGOR
- Author every pixel natively at 96×64. No upscale-and-clean, no downscale-and-process.
- One-pixel solid outline in ink (#1A1612). No anti-aliasing on silhouette. No semi-transparent silhouette pixels.
- ≤ 18 unique colors per frame; ≤ 32 unique colors over the whole sheet.
- Dither: 1px ordered only, on the back/shoulder transition where fur catches light. Never spray noise on the body.
- 12fps animation cadence. Lunge frames hold 80ms (faster than other characters — combat reads as quick); idle holds 200ms+ on the breath frame.
- The wolf's eye is 1 pixel — never larger. No catchlight, no white sclera.

PALETTE (use these tokens; do not introduce others)
- Fur main:        damp-ash #6B6358 (base) · pine-shadow #2A3329 (deep / spine line) · vellum-bone #E8DFC9 (belly highlight, ≤ 6 px per frame)
- Fur shadow:      ink-soft #2B2520 (under-belly shadow, ear-pinned shadow)
- Eye:             ember-red #C63E1F (1 px, idle/walk/lunge/bite frames) · ink #1A1612 (1 px, hit-react/flee/death frames — the light goes out)
- Mouth / nose:    ink #1A1612
- Tongue (bite frame only): dragon-red #8A2018, ≤ 3 px
- Pads / claws:    ink #1A1612, 1–2 px clusters
- Ground shadow under wolf: damp-ash #6B6358 with 2px ordered dither at 50% density, never solid

OUTPUT
- File: `art/characters/wolves/wolf_full_sheet_96x64.png`
- One transparent-PNG sprite sheet meeting every constraint above.
- Empty cells fully transparent (alpha 0, all channels).

ACCEPTANCE
1. Sheet is exactly 480 × 512 px.
2. Every applicable frame's paws sit on y = 60 within its cell. Lunge frames 2–3 (mid-air) anchor by implied trajectory — landing frame 4 paws return to y = 60.
3. ≤ 32 unique colors over the whole sheet.
4. Silhouette is single-pixel outlined; no semi-transparent silhouette pixels.
5. At 1× pixel scale, a single 96×64 cell is recognizable as a wolf — long-bodied quadruped with bushy tail, ears, snout. Not a dog. Not a fantasy direwolf.
6. The lunge sequence reads as quick and dangerous through pose alone — no motion blur, no speedlines, no glow.
7. The death frame is recoverable-body: clearly dead but not gory; this is the Act 5 loot eligibility pose, and `loot.wolf.material` events fire from this state.
8. The eye-light shift (ember-red #C63E1F → ink #1A1612 between living and downed states) is the one moment where the project's "earned colors" rule allows red on a non-tincture-vial sprite. Use it sparingly: 1 pixel.

REFERENCES (anatomy and tone guide ONLY)
- `design_system/v0_9_mercy_rpg_substrate/03-opening-act-bible.md` §Act 5 — encounter data, threat targets, loot/material eligibility, debug acceptance
- `design_system/v0_9_mercy_rpg_substrate/05-rpg-economy-progression.md` — wolf material consequence (rough hide / sinew / tooth/claw / meat) and quality/rarity rules
- `design_system/colors_and_type.css` — palette tokens
- `docs/adr/0008-wolf-combat-is-violent-and-lootable.md` — combat is real; loot is grounded; objective is boy safety

Do not imitate any living artist. Return one PNG.
```
